ORIGIN OF THE WORD “COCKTAIL”

There is debate about when the term was first used. According the The London Telegraph , the word is first found in print in a March 20, 1798 satirical newspaper article.

Others point to an April 28, 1803 article from The Farmer’s Cabinet in Vermont, where to drink a cocktail was claimed to be “excellent for the head.”

Regardless, certainly by 1806, the word was being used with its current meaning. “a stimulating liquor composed of spirits of any kind – sugar, water, and bitters.”

During the early 17th century, an animal, particularly a horse, with a docked tail, was said to have a “cock tail.”
